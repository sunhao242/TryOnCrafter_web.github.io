Abstract

Video virtual try-on has made strong progress in synthesizing realistic garment overlays on dynamic subjects, yet existing methods remain constrained by the camera trajectory of the input monocular video. TryOnCrafter introduces Camera-controllable Video Virtual Try-on (CaM-VVT), a new setting that enables virtual fitting under arbitrary camera movements. The method builds a Renderable 4D Try-on Proxy by distilling 2D try-on priors into a clothed 3D Gaussian avatar, animating it with SMPL-X motion, and aligning it with a reconstructed background point cloud. A Proxy-Anchored Video DiT then uses the rendered proxy sequence, cross-view reference features, and multimodal semantic conditions to synthesize photorealistic try-on videos with consistent garment identity, plausible deformation, and coherent scene structure.

Highlights

Camera-controllable VVT

TryOnCrafter extends video virtual try-on beyond passive replay, allowing synthesis under user-specified camera paths such as zoom, tilt, and orbit.

Renderable 4D Try-on Proxy

The proxy decouples the clothed human from the environment and provides dense geometric guidance for viewpoint-consistent video generation.

Proxy-Anchored Video DiT

A diffusion transformer leverages rendered priors, reference features, and semantic garment cues to preserve appearance under novel trajectories.

Method Overview

Overall pipeline figure placeholder

4D Proxy Construction

TryOnCrafter reconstructs the scene, separates human and background, aligns SMPL-X motion into world space, and generates a target-garment 3DGS avatar from a selected keyframe.

Proxy-Anchored Generation

The rendered proxy video is injected as a pixel-aligned structural prior, constraining the DiT to follow the requested camera path while preserving body motion and garment geometry.

Reference and Semantic Cues

The Cross-view Reference Adapter and CLIP/UmT5 conditions supply fine-grained identity, material, texture, and scene-level semantic information.
