Abstract

TryOnCrafter introduces Camera-controllable Video Virtual Try-on (CaM-VVT), moving video try-on beyond the fixed camera path of the input monocular video.

Existing video virtual try-on methods can synthesize realistic garment overlays on dynamic subjects, but they remain tied to the source camera trajectory. TryOnCrafter addresses this limitation with a unified DiT-based framework for camera-controllable video virtual try-on. The method constructs a renderable 4D try-on proxy by distilling high-fidelity 2D try-on priors into a clothed 3D Gaussian avatar, animating it with SMPL-X motion, and aligning it with a reconstructed background point cloud. A proxy-anchored video diffusion transformer then uses rendered priors, cross-view reference features, and multimodal semantic conditions to synthesize photorealistic try-on videos under arbitrary camera trajectories while preserving garment identity, subject motion, and scene coherence.

Method

The framework combines explicit 4D geometric reconstruction with diffusion-based video synthesis, using the renderable proxy as a dense structural anchor.

Stage 01

4D Try-on Proxy

Reconstructs the scene, separates human and background, aligns SMPL-X motion into world space, and builds a clothed 3DGS avatar from a target-garment reference.

Stage 02

Proxy-Anchored DiT

Uses rendered proxy video and masks as pixel-aligned priors, constraining the generation to the prescribed camera trajectory and physically plausible motion.

Stage 03

Cross-view Fidelity

A Cross-view Reference Adapter and CLIP/UmT5 semantic tokens recover source identity, garment material cues, and global scene consistency in unseen views.

Overall pipeline / architecture figure placeholder

Anchor-based Alignment

A confidence-aware point-to-surface objective maps SMPL-X vertices from camera space to the reconstructed world space, reducing scale and coordinate ambiguity.

Deformation & Rendering

The canonical clothed 3DGS avatar is driven by SMPL-X through linear blend skinning, then rendered together with the background under input or novel trajectories.

Rendered Prior Injection

The rendered video is encoded and concatenated with latent noise, giving the DiT dense guidance for global geometry, body pose, and garment deformation.

Cross-view Reference Adapter

CRA shares key-value projections with the DiT backbone while using independent query/output weights, injecting reference residuals without destabilizing the prior.

Results

Experiments cover standard video virtual try-on and the new CaM-VVTBench for camera-controllable try-on with tilt, zoom, and orbit trajectories.

On ViViD, TryOnCrafter improves video-level fidelity and perceptual quality across paired and unpaired protocols. On CaM-VVTBench, it achieves the best overall score among two-stage camera-control baselines, with stronger subject consistency, imaging quality, and overall consistency under complex camera maneuvers.

Applications

Because the 4D proxy disentangles the human subject, garment, motion, and background, it naturally supports interactive camera and scene edits.
